The Glass Pavilion

Malibu, California

Perched on the Malibu cliffs, this 4,200 square foot residence dissolves the traditional boundary between interior space and the Pacific landscape beyond. Floor-to-ceiling glass walls frame ever-changing views of ocean, sky, and coastline.

Concept

The design emerged from a simple observation: the most profound architectural experiences often come from the interplay of light and material. Here, we sought to create a dwelling that would be in constant dialogue with its environment— a pavilion of glass and stone that celebrates the California coast while providing intimate shelter.

The structure is organized around a central courtyard, with living spaces arranged to capture morning light from the east and evening sunsets to the west. Cantilevered roof planes provide shade while maintaining unobstructed views.

Materials

A restrained palette of materials—board-formed concrete, white oak, and floor-to-ceiling low-iron glass—creates a sense of calm continuity throughout. The concrete walls bear the subtle texture of their formwork, grounding the lightness of the glass pavilions.
